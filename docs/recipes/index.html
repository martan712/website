Curry

Ingredients

  • 2 onions
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 25g fresh ginger
  • 2 eggplants
  • 500g (+100 possibly) cooking potatoes
  • 400g chickpeas
  • sunflower oil
  • paprikapowder (+/- 1 tsp)
  • ground kurkuma (+/- 2-3 tsp)
  • 1 cube golden curry
  • chiliflakes
  • 400g peeled/diced tomatoes
  • 500-600ml water
  • peanuts (*)
  • arabisch bread (*)
  • yoghurt (*)

* optional

Instructions

Prep:

  • Dice eggplant into cubes of ~1cm.
  • Finely chop the onions.
  • Cut the garlic into fine slices.
  • Peel the ginger with a spoon and cut into ass small cubes as possibl.e (cut along the long side until almost the top, then dice over short).
  • Clean potatos, peel them and cut into cubes of ~1cm.
  • Drain the chickpeas and let some cold flowing water drain through it.

Cooking:

  • Bake the eggplant in suflower oil for about 8 minutes until soft and golden-brown. Toss around every once in a while and bring to flavour with salt and pepper.
  • Fruit the onion for about 5 min, add 1-2 teaspoons of sambal to fry along with it.
  • Add the eggplant, ginger, garlic, potato-cubes, chickpeas, paprikapowder and krukuma, and cook for 1-2 minutes while mixing. Make sure everything is mixed well!
  • Add the tomatoes and the water (all of both or until everything is about covered). Then crumble the cube of golden curry into the mixture. Bring to a boil and leave with the lid on to cook for +/-25 min or until the potato is soft. Mix every once in a while to make sure nothing is sticking/burning to the sides or bottom!
  • Crush a few of the potatoes and tomatoes against the sides/bottom until the curry takes form. Now leave it to cook without lid until it has thickened enough (about 10 min).
  • Add some chili flakes until you feel it is spicy enough, then bring to taste with salt and pepper.

While cooking:

  • Bake some peanuts during the 25 minutes of steaming. Bake wihout oil in the pan and until browned.
  • Put them inside a mug or cup, and mix them generously with salt, and after add some salt crystals that are freshly cracked.
  • Place in fridge or freezer. They have to be (have been) cold before serving to have a nice crunch!

Plating:

  • Add curry to deep plate or bowl.
  • Add peanuts on top and/or next to it.
  • Add some freshly chopped coriander, or coriander leaf spices on top.
  • Add some sambal to the side.
  • Best served with Arabic bread and yoghurt.
